A Few Words About Us

McLaren Automotive (formerly known as McLaren Cars) is a British automotive manufacturer founded by Ron Dennis and is based at the McLaren Technology Campus in Woking, Surrey. The main products of the company are sports cars with elements of luxury and everyday practicability, usually produced in-house at designated production facilities. In July 2017, McLaren Automotive became 100% owned by the wider McLaren Technology Group.

More about us

McLaren launched its three-tier product structure in 2015, introducing a new naming strategy that includes range names (Sports, Super and Ultimate Series) and derivatives (LT, S and C) followed by power output in PS. The move intends to take McLaren through its next decade of growth. The entry-level Sports Series consists of the 570S Coupé, Spider, 570GT and the 540C Coupé; the Super Series, the core McLaren model range, is made up of the 675LT and 650S which are now replaced by the 720S; and the most exclusive McLaren range is the Ultimate Series, led by the McLaren P1 and P1 GTR and now by the McLaren Senna along with the upcoming BP23.
